The 2025 Measles Outbreak: A Wake-Up Call

The Spike We Can’t Ignore

In early 2025, measles cases began climbing rapidly across the United States. Week after week, the numbers kept rising — bringing back a virus once considered nearly eliminated.


Texas at the Center of It All

By spring 2025, one state accounted for a disproportionate share of cases: Texas. The outbreak was not evenly spread — some counties were hit much harder than others


Who Is Getting Sick?

Children are at the frontlines of this outbreak. Nearly half of all reported cases in Texas were among kids aged 0–17 — including infants too young to be vaccinated.


The Vaccination Gap

This outbreak isn’t random. Over 90% of the people infected in Texas were unvaccinated or had unknown vaccination status.


How Did We Get Here?

Vaccines nearly wiped out measles after their introduction in 1963. But in recent years, MMR coverage has been slipping — below the threshold needed for herd immunity.


Impact: Lives Lost

Three children have died in this outbreak. All were under age 5. None were vaccinated. These were preventable deaths.


The Herd Protects the Vulnerable

Measles is one of the most contagious viruses on earth — but it’s also entirely preventable. Vaccination doesn't just protect you. It protects babies, the immunocompromised, and your community
